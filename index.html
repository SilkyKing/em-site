Why I'm doing this

I want our child to dream in two languages.

I grew up speaking Dutch. It's the language my mother sang to me in, the one my whole childhood lives inside. Now we're raising our family on a tropical island far from home, and there's a quiet fear every bilingual parent knows: that the language slips away one ordinary day at a time, and the child never gets it back.

Children don't learn a language from apps or flashcards. They learn it from a person — from being talked to, sung to, read to, every single day. So I'm looking for someone who can simply be that person for an hour a day. That's the whole idea. It's small, and it's everything.

What life here looks like

Mornings slow, days warm, the ocean close.

  • Your own room in our villa, housing covered — a real home, not a guest you're rushing out of.
  • A tropical island. Lombok is Bali's quieter, greener neighbour — rice fields, surf, waterfalls, warm people.
  • A family, not a job. Shared meals, easy mornings, the kind of days that feel like a long warm exhale.
  • Time and space of your own. The exchange is light by design. The rest of your days are yours.

The exchange, honestly

It's a small thing I'm asking. On purpose.

What I'm offering

A room in our villa on the island, with housing covered, and a place inside a real family for as long as it's a good fit.

What I'm asking

Roughly an hour a day, speaking Dutch with our little one — playing, reading, singing. Warmth, presence, and consistency. That's it.
